About this game

Lel.tar is an arcade-style "shmup" game, heavily inspired by games like Touhou Project and QP Shooting. It features simple graphics, guaranteed to run smooth enough even on weaker integrated GPUs (i.e. Intel® HD/UHD Graphics); as well as the same engaging gameplay carefully designed for any kind of player.

Backstory / Prologue

You went to order a package from your favourite shopping site, SchmitarMarkt. The delivery man from OOPS comes to place it in front of your door. You come and get it, and- uh oh! Some shady porch pirates come to beat you up and steal the package from you, and then they fly away to their organization's (DICS) warehouse to then maybe resell it. Having experienced that, as well as hearing news of other locals being affected by those guys, it's time to fly up and follow them to the warehouse to retrieve the package(s) once and for all!

Technical details

This game was made using Godot Engine v4.5.1 using the GDScript language. It supports native Vulkan rendering, with fallbacks for DirectX 12 and OpenGL 3 if not supported by the hardware.

Compatibility with Wine and Proton should be quite good if you choose to play the game this way, especially on macOS.

System requirements

Minimum

  • OS: Windows 10 (or equivalent Linux and/or Wine version)
  • CPU: x86_64 CPU with SSE4.2 support
  • RAM: 4 GB
  • GPU: Support for OpenGL 3.3 (or newer/equivalent)
  • Storage: 150 MB of free space

Recommended

  • OS: Windows 10 (or equivalent Linux and/or Wine version)
  • CPU: Intel® Core™ i7-3770 (or equivalent)
  • RAM: 8 GB
  • GPU: Support for Vulkan 1.2 (or newer)
  • Storage: 250 MB of free space or more
